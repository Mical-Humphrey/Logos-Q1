1. Moving Average (SMA)

The simple moving average smooths recent prices to reveal the underlying trend.

$$ SMA_t = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} Price_{t-i} $$

2. Standard Deviation

Measures volatility — how far prices deviate from their mean.

$$ \sigma_t = \sqrt{\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=0}^{N-1}(Price_{t-i} - SMA_t)^2} $$

3. Z-Score

How many standard deviations the current price is from the mean.

$$ z_t = \frac{Price_t - SMA_t}{\sigma_t} $$

4. Correlation and Hedge Ratio

Correlation measures the strength of co-movement between two assets.

$$ \rho_{A,B} = \frac{Cov(A,B)}{\sigma_A \sigma_B} $$

Regression-based hedge ratio:

$$ A = \alpha + \beta B + \epsilon $$

5. Sharpe Ratio

Risk-adjusted return — compares average excess return to volatility.

$$ Sharpe = \frac{E[R - R_f]}{\sigma_R} $$

6. CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)

Average annual rate of return compounded over time.

$$ CAGR = \left(\frac{V_{end}}{V_{start}}\right)^{\frac{1}{T}} - 1 $$

7. Maximum Drawdown

Largest peak-to-trough drop in portfolio equity.

$$ MDD = \frac{Min(Equity)}{Max(Equity_{prior})} - 1 $$

8. Exposure

Fraction of total time a strategy maintains open positions.

$$ Exposure = \frac{Time_{in\ Market}}{Total\ Time} $$

9. Slippage (Basis Points)

Execution friction: deviation between ideal and actual fill prices.

$$ Price_{fill} = Price_{signal} \times \left(1 + Side \times \frac{bps}{10,000}\right) $$

10. Position Sizing

Determine how many shares to trade given fixed dollar allocation.

$$ Shares = \text{floor}\left(\frac{DollarPerTrade}{Price}\right) $$

11. Regression-Based Mean Reversion

The spread between two correlated assets reverts to its mean over time.

$$ Spread_t = \alpha + \rho Spread_{t-1} + \epsilon_t $$

If |ρ| < 1, mean reversion behavior is expected.
