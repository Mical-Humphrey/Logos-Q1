This guide teaches the core mathematics used by Logos-Q1. Each formula block includes: a LaTeX formula, a Variables list so \(N\), \(P_t\), \(\sigma_t\), etc. are obvious, a plain-English intuition, and three worked examples tuned to the active context.

Common Variables

\(t\): current bar index (time) \(N\): lookback window length (bars) \(P_t\): price at time \(t\) (close or mid) \(\overline{P}_t\): moving average at \(t\) \(\sigma_t\): standard deviation at \(t\) \(s_t\): trading signal \(\in\{-1,0,+1\}\) \(V_t\): volume at \(t\) \(E_t\): equity (portfolio value) at \(t\) \(R_t\): portfolio return at \(t\) \(R_f\): risk-free return (periodic) \(T\): years elapsed bps: basis points (1 bps = 0.01%)

Context: US equities (e.g., MSFT, AAPL, TSLA). Daily bars, standard hours, splits/dividends possible.

1) Simple Moving Average (SMA)

Formula

$$ \mathrm{SMA}_t = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=0}^{N-1} P_{t-i} $$

Variables: \(P_t\) price at time \(t\); \(N\) window length (e.g., 20 days).

Intuition: Smooths noise to reveal trend.

  • MSFT: Price closes above rising 20-day SMA after pullback ‚Üí bullish continuation.
  • AAPL: 50d and 200d SMAs ‚Äî the ‚ÄúGolden Cross‚Äù is 50 over 200.
  • TSLA: 5d vs 20d SMA helps time volatile swings; size down if volatility is high.

2) Exponential Moving Average (EMA)

Formula

$$ \mathrm{EMA}_t = \alpha P_t + (1-\alpha)\,\mathrm{EMA}_{t-1}, \quad \alpha=\frac{2}{N+1} $$

Variables: \(\alpha\) smoothing factor; \(N\) EMA span; \(P_t\) price.

Intuition: Reacts faster than SMA.

  • MSFT: 12‚Äì26 EMA pair catches post-earnings drift.
  • AAPL: 9‚Äì21 EMA cross for swing entries.
  • TSLA: 10 EMA ‚Äúrides‚Äù strong trends; beware whipsaws.

3) Standard Deviation (Volatility)

Formula

$$ \sigma_t = \sqrt{\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=0}^{N-1}\left(P_{t-i}-\overline{P}_t\right)^2} $$

Variables: \(\overline{P}_t\) = SMA at \(t\); \(N\) bars.

Intuition: Typical distance from mean; higher \(\sigma\) = choppier market.

  • MSFT: \(\sigma\approx 1\%\) ‚Üí calm conditions; wider stops unnecessary.
  • AAPL: \(\sigma\approx 2\%\) ‚Üí moderate chop; adjust risk.
  • TSLA: \(\sigma\approx 4\%-5\%\) ‚Üí very volatile; smaller positions.

4) Z-Score (How Extreme is Price?)

Formula

$$ Z_t = \frac{P_t - \overline{P}_t}{\sigma_t} $$

Variables: \(P_t\) price; \(\overline{P}_t\) SMA; \(\sigma_t\) std dev.

Intuition: Standardized deviation from the mean; thresholds like \(|Z|\ge 2\) are common.

  • MSFT: \(Z=+2.1\) ‚Üí extended; mean reversion likely.
  • AAPL: \(Z=-1.8\) ‚Üí near oversold zone.
  • TSLA: \(Z=+3\) ‚Üí extreme; caution on momentum chasing.

5) Momentum: SMA Crossover Signal

Signal

$$ s_t = \mathrm{sign}\!\left(\mathrm{SMA}^{\text{fast}}_t - \mathrm{SMA}^{\text{slow}}_t\right)\in\{-1,0,+1\} $$

Variables: Fast vs slow SMA windows; \(s_t\) signal.

Intuition: Fast above slow ‚Üí long bias; below ‚Üí short bias.

  • MSFT: 20/50 cross reduces noise, gives steadier trends.
  • AAPL: 10/30 for responsive swing trades.
  • TSLA: 5/20 is aggressive; pair with tighter stops.

6) VWAP (Volume-Weighted Average Price)

Formula

$$ \mathrm{VWAP} = \frac{\sum_i P_i V_i}{\sum_i V_i} $$

Variables: \(P_i\) trade/bar price; \(V_i\) volume.

Intuition: Execution benchmark; ‚Äúfair‚Äù average price weighted by volume.

  • MSFT: Price above VWAP = demand; below = supply.
  • AAPL: Measuring slippage vs daily VWAP is common.
  • TSLA: Trend days often ride above VWAP all session.

7) Sharpe Ratio (Risk-Adjusted Return)

Formula

$$ \mathrm{Sharpe} = \frac{\mathbb{E}[R_p - R_f]}{\sigma_p} $$

Variables: \(R_p\) periodic portfolio returns; \(R_f\) risk-free; \(\sigma_p\) std dev of \(R_p\). Annualize with \(\sqrt{252}\) for daily.

  • MSFT strat: mean 0.08% daily, \(\sigma=1\%\) ‚Üí Sharpe \(\approx 1.26\).
  • AAPL strat: mean 0.10%, \(\sigma=1.2\%\) ‚Üí \(\approx 1.32\).
  • TSLA strat: mean 0.15%, \(\sigma=2.5\%\) ‚Üí \(\approx 0.95\).

8) CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)

Formula

$$ \mathrm{CAGR} = \left(\frac{E_{\text{end}}}{E_{\text{start}}}\right)^{1/T} - 1 $$

Variables: Equity start/end; \(T\) years.

  • MSFT: 100k ‚Üí 120k in 2y ‚Üí 9.5%.
  • AAPL: 100k ‚Üí 140k in 3y ‚Üí 11.9%.
  • TSLA: 100k ‚Üí 250k in 3y ‚Üí 35.7%.

9) Max Drawdown (Peak-to-Trough Loss)

Formula

$$ \mathrm{MDD} = \min_t\left(\frac{E_t}{\max_{s\le t} E_s} - 1\right) $$

Variables: \(E_t\) equity, running peak \(\max_{s\le t} E_s\).

  • MSFT: ‚Äì15% during rotation.
  • AAPL: ‚Äì22% post-earnings drift.
  • TSLA: ‚Äì50% in 2022 ‚Äî manage size.

10) Slippage & Position Sizing

Fill model

$$ P_{\text{fill}} = P_{\text{signal}}\left(1 + \mathrm{side}\cdot\frac{\mathrm{bps}}{10{,}000}\right) $$

Variables: \(\mathrm{side}\in\{+1,-1\}\); bps = basis points.

Shares

$$ \mathrm{Shares}=\left\lfloor \frac{\$\,\text{per trade}}{P_{\text{fill}}} \right\rfloor $$

  • MSFT: $10k at $320, 1 bps ‚Üí $320.032 ‚Üí 31 sh.
  • AAPL: $10k at $170, 3 bps ‚Üí $170.051 ‚Üí 58 sh.
  • TSLA: $10k at $250, 10 bps ‚Üí $250.25 ‚Üí 39 sh.

Context: Crypto (BTC-USD, ETH-USD, DOGE-USD). 24/7 trading, higher volatility, exchange fees & variable depth.

5) Momentum: MA Crossovers

Signal

$$ s_t = \mathrm{sign}\!\left(\mathrm{MA}^{\text{fast}}_t - \mathrm{MA}^{\text{slow}}_t\right) $$

Variables: MA can be SMA or EMA; define fast & slow windows.

Intuition: Favor sustained moves; suppress noise.

  • BTC: 20h/50h avoids micro churn.
  • ETH: 10h/30h for responsiveness.
  • DOGE: 50h/200h for regime identification.

6) VWAP (Execution Benchmark)

Formula

$$ \mathrm{VWAP} = \frac{\sum_i P_i V_i}{\sum_i V_i} $$

Variables: \(P_i\) price; \(V_i\) volume.

Intuition: Aim to buy below and sell above VWAP on range days.

  • BTC: Oscillates around hourly VWAP in ranges.
  • ETH: Persistent premium above VWAP indicates strong demand.
  • DOGE: VWAP can prevent late-stage chasing.

7) Sharpe (24/7 Annualization)

Formula

Variables: For hourly bars, \(\sqrt{365\cdot 24}\); for daily, \(\sqrt{365}\).

  • BTC: Hourly Sharpe uses \(\sqrt{8760}\).
  • ETH: Daily Sharpe uses \(\sqrt{365}\).
  • DOGE: Use longer windows to stabilize \(\sigma_R\).

10) Fees, Slippage & Sizing

Fee

$$ \mathrm{Fee}_{\$} = \mathrm{Notional}\times\frac{\mathrm{bps}}{10{,}000} $$

Slippage Fill

$$ P_{\text{fill}} = P_{\text{signal}}\left(1+\mathrm{side}\cdot\frac{\mathrm{slip\_bps}}{10{,}000}\right) $$

Variables: Notional = \(|q|\cdot P_{\text{fill}}\); side ¬±1; bps.

Units

$$ \text{Units}=\left\lfloor \frac{\$\,\text{per trade}}{P_{\text{fill}}} \right\rfloor $$

  • BTC: 5 bps fee on $10k notional ‚Üí $5 cost; consider fractional sizing.
  • ETH: 2 bps maker ‚Üí $4 cost on $20k; slippage still applies.
  • DOGE: Depth varies ‚Äî slippage can dominate costs.

10) Pip, Spread, and Effective Fill & Sizing

Pip size: most pairs \(=0.0001\); JPY pairs \(=0.01\).

Effective fill

$$ P_{\text{eff}} = P_{\text{mid}} + \mathrm{side}\times \text{spread\_pips}\times \text{pip\_size} $$

Variables: mid price; side ¬±1; spread in pips; pip_size.

Units

$$ \text{Units}=\left\lfloor \frac{\$\,\text{per trade}}{P_{\text{eff}}} \right\rfloor \qquad \text{Lots}=\frac{\text{Units}}{100{,}000} $$

  • EUR/USD: mid 1.0950, spread 1 pip ‚Üí buy eff 1.0951; $10k budget ‚Üí ~9,132 units (‚âà0.091 lots).
  • USD/JPY: mid 155.20, spread 0.5 pip ‚Üí buy eff 155.205; sizing often via broker‚Äôs margin calc.
  • GBP/USD: mid 1.2700, spread 2 pips ‚Üí sell eff 1.2698; adjust for leverage and risk per trade.
