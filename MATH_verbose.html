Context: US equities (e.g., MSFT, AAPL). Daily bars, trading hours, corporate actions.

1) Simple Moving Average (SMA)

Formula

$$ SMA_t = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} Price_{t-i} $$

Intuition: SMA smooths noisy prices to reveal trend.

Example (Equities): Over the last N=5 days, prices were [100, 101, 99, 100, 102].
The average is 100.4. If today's price 102 is far above SMA, the short-term trend is up.

In Logos-Q1: Momentum uses SMAs to create crossover signals.

2) Standard Deviation (Volatility)

Formula

$$ \sigma_t = \sqrt{\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=0}^{N-1}(Price_{t-i} - SMA_t)^2} $$

Intuition: Measures typical distance from mean; higher σ = choppier market.

Example (Equities): If σ ≈ 1.1, a ±2σ move is about 2.2 in price terms.

In Logos-Q1: Mean reversion uses σ to compute z-scores and thresholds.

3) Z-Score (How Extreme is Price?)

Formula

$$ z_t = \frac{Price_t - SMA_t}{\sigma_t} $$

Intuition: Number of standard deviations current price is from its mean.

Example (Equities): If current price = 102, SMA = 100.4, σ = 1.1, then $$ z = \frac{102 - 100.4}{1.1} \approx 1.45. $$ z < -2 ⇒ statistically cheap; z > +2 ⇒ statistically rich.

In Logos-Q1: Mean reversion enters when z crosses ±z_entry.

4) Correlation & Pairs Trading

Correlation: $$ \rho_{A,B} = \frac{Cov(A,B)}{\sigma_A \sigma_B} $$

Hedge Ratio (β) via regression: $$ A = \alpha + \beta B + \epsilon $$

Example (Equities): If MSFT and AAPL move together (ρ ≈ 0.9), estimate β and trade the spread: $$ Spread = Price_{MSFT} - \beta\,Price_{AAPL}. $$ When z-score of spread ≤ -2 ⇒ long MSFT / short AAPL. Reverse when ≥ +2.

In Logos-Q1: See src/strategies/pairs_trading.py.

5) Sharpe Ratio (Risk-Adjusted Return)

Formula

$$ Sharpe = \frac{E[R - R_f]}{\sigma_R} $$

Example (Equities): If daily mean return is 0.08% and daily σ is 1%, annualized Sharpe ≈ $$ \frac{0.0008}{0.01} \times \sqrt{252} \approx 1.27. $$

In Logos-Q1: Computed from equity curve daily returns.

6) CAGR, Drawdown, Exposure

CAGR: $$ CAGR = \left(\frac{V_{end}}{V_{start}}\right)^{1/T} - 1 $$

Max Drawdown: $$ MDD = \min_t\left(\frac{Equity_t}{\max_{s\le t} Equity_s} - 1\right) $$

Exposure: $$ \frac{Time\ in\ Market}{Total\ Time} $$

Example (Equities): If equity went from 100k → 121k over 2 years, CAGR = (1.21)^(1/2)-1 ≈ 10%. If deepest drop from peak was -12%, MaxDD = -0.12. If the system held positions 70% of days, Exposure = 0.70.

In Logos-Q1: See src/backtest/metrics.py.

7) Slippage & Position Sizing

Slippage (bps): $$ Price_{fill} = Price_{signal}\,\left(1 + Side\,\tfrac{bps}{10,000}\right) $$

Sizing: $$ Shares = \left\lfloor \tfrac{DollarPerTrade}{Price} \right\rfloor $$

Example (Equities): With $10,000 per trade at price 102, you trade $$ \left\lfloor \tfrac{10000}{102} \right\rfloor = 98 \text{ shares}. $$ With 1 bps slippage on a buy, fill ≈ 102.0102.
